How Songs Are Scored

The Rarity
System

Every song gets a rarity tier based on how often it appears in setlists. Here's exactly how we calculate it.

Staple
Tier 1 — Staple
Played at the vast majority of shows. Near-certainties at any concert.
Regular
Tier 2 — Regular
Frequent but not guaranteed. A solid rotation pick at most tours.
Uncommon
Tier 3 — Uncommon
Appears occasionally. A pleasant surprise when it shows up.
Rare
Tier 4 — Rare
Seldom played. Fans go years between hearings. A real moment.
Ultra Rare
Tier 5 — Ultra Rare
Played a handful of times ever. A once-in-a-decade event.

How We Calculate

Primary Score
Rarity Score = total_plays ÷ total_shows_with_setlists
Tag Weight
Tags (brief teases/snippets) count as 0.5 plays — not a full performance

What counts as a "play"? A song appearing as a standalone setlist entry — full performance, debut, or cover. Songs only teased or quoted briefly are stored as tags on their host song and weighted at half credit.

Tiers are percentile buckets across all 553 songs with at least one documented play. Songs with zero plays and no album association are hidden from the app entirely.

Scores update whenever new shows are added. A song's tier can shift over time as Pearl Jam's setlist habits evolve across tours.

Show Eligibility

What Counts
as a Show?

The 1,129 shows in Red Mosquito follow clear inclusion rules. Here's exactly what's in and what's out.

Counted

  • ✅Full Pearl Jam headlining concerts — any venue, any size, including early Mookie Blaylock-era shows (first show: 10/22/90)
  • ✅Festival sets where Pearl Jam performed a substantial setlist
  • ✅Fan club (Ten Club) shows, including late-night and warm-up sets
  • ✅TV tapings and broadcast performances that included live music
  • ✅Benefit shows and one-off charity performances
  • ✅Album release events with live performances
  • ✅Shows where the setlist is partially known or reconstructed from reliable sources

Not Counted

  • ❌Solo or side-project performances by individual band members
  • ❌Rehearsal sessions and soundchecks, even if partially recorded
  • ❌Guest appearances by Pearl Jam members at another artist's show
  • ❌Studio sessions or acoustic in-stores without a live setlist
  • ❌Planned shows that were cancelled with no performance
  • ❌Performances by Pearl Jam members in other bands or solo projects where Pearl Jam as a unit was not performing
On Setlist Completeness: Of the 1,129 shows in the database, 1,039 have fully documented setlists verified against PJ.com. The remaining 90 shows are included for browsing and attendance tracking, but flagged as incomplete. Song play counts and rarity scores are calculated only from verified setlist entries.
